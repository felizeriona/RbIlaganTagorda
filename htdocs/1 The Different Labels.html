The Different Labels

About the Subpage

This section discusses the different labels, sexualities, and identities under the umbrella term LGBTQIA+. Note that this is neither a complete nor extensive list as many other identities develop and may change terms as time passes. To be safe, simply ask people what they want to be referred to as and what label they are under.


Difference between Sexual Orientation and Gender

Sexual orientation defines a person’s attraction to others, be it physical, emotional, or romantic. On the other hand, gender is how a person views themselves internally, whether feminine, masculine or in between, through their sensation of their body, speech, mannerisms, clothing, etc.

Lesbian A woman who is emotionally, romantically or sexually attracted to other women
Gay A person who is emotionally, romantically or sexually attracted to people of the same sex;
commonly used to describe men
Bisexual A person who is emotionally or sexually attracted to more than one gender
Pansexual A person who can be attracted to anyone, regardless of their gender identity
Transgender An umbrella term for a person whose gender identity differs from the sex they were assigned at birth
Non-Binary A person whose gender is in between or outside the gender binary of male and female
It may be fixed or fluid—changing over time
Intersex A person whose biological sex is in between or outside the binary of male and female
It may be a mix of the two traits or none at all
Asexual An umbrella term for a person who experiences little to no sexual attraction
They may experience it only under given circumstances or never at all
Aromantic An umbrella term for a person who experiences little to no romantic attraction
They may experience it only under given circumstances or never at all
