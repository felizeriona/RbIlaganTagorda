Media & Materials for Awareness

About the Subpage

This section provides reliable sources in the form of websites where people can get additional and accurate information on the community. There are also movies and TV shows listed below with a focus on LGBTQIA+.


Websites

Queerdom Wiki is dedicated to spreading awareness on the different labels of the community but is primarily focused on the micro-labels or the lesser-known ones.


The Center is the backbone of the queer community in New York. It is focused on helping the community with programs, resources, and providing them with connections to other queer people.


The Safe Zone Project focuses on providing information on SOGIE. They have educators who are willing to teach and hold activities for its many learners.


LGBT Foundation acts as a support for each and every member of the community. They build communities for each member to help one another grow and maintain their wellbeing. They promote security within the community for the future of all.


Movies

The Panti Sisters (2019)

A wealthy father diagnosed with terminal cancer calls back his 3 gay sons with an offer: PHP300 million for the first to give him a grandchild.


Billie and Emma (2018)

In a rural all-girls Catholic school, the ambitious star student (Emma) falls in love with the new girl (Billie), an aspiring rockstar who stands out with her short hair and combat boots. Their blossoming relationship runs into an obstacle when Emma discovers she is pregnant.


Everything Everywhere All at Once (2022)

While under IRS audit, a Chinese American immigrant discovers she can travel between parallel dimensions by connecting with the versions of herself residing within these worlds. She must use her new powers to defeat a powerful entity that threatens to destroy the multiverse.


Love, Simon (2018)

A comedic coming-of-age story is centered on 17-year old Simon Spier. He is gay but no one knows it, not his family or his friends. He has also fallen in love with an anonymous classmate online. Resolving the two proves to change Simon's life forever.


TV Shows

Bloom Into You Written & Illlustrated by Nakatani Nio

Yuu has always adored shoujo manga and looks forward to the day she receives a love confession, but when a classmate in junior high professes his love to her, she is indifferent. Yuu enters high school disappointed and perplexed, yet unsure of how to react. At that moment, Yuu encounters the charming student council president Nanami decline a suitor in such a mature manner that she is motivated to seek her guidance. What if Nanami herself is the next person to confess to Yuu?


The Owl House Created by Dana Terrace

A confident teenaged human girl, Luz, unintentionally discovers a gateway to the Boiling Isles. She becomes friends with a disobedient witch named Eda and a little warrior named King there. Luz, who lacks magical powers, works as Eda's apprentice at the Owl House to fulfill her dream of becoming a witch. She eventually finds her new family in an unexpected place.


Arcane Created by Christian Linke and Alex Yee

Arcane delves into the delicate balance between the downtrodden subterranean of Zaun and the utopian city of Piltover, which is based on the universe of League of Legends. Many of the brightest minds reside in these cities, which are known across Runeterra as the "cities of progress." However, the development of hextech, a method for anybody to harness magical energy, endangers this equilibrium. 


The Sandman Created by Neil Gaiman

The adventures of the god of dreams—Dream of the Endless— in realms outside of our comprehension; a s he corrects the cosmic and human errors he has committed over his lengthy existence, the Sandman pursues the people and places the Dream King has affected. It tells the tale of how Morpheus is captured and discovers that sometimes transformation is unavoidable.


Fiction Books

Red, White & Royal Blue by Casey McQuiston

The First Son of the White House (Alex Claremont-Diaz) and Britain's Prince of Wales (Prince Henry) are forced to act as best friends after an international scandal. The pair start off as begrudging friends but soon find themselves on the verge of something more.


7 Husbands of Evelyn Hugo by Taylor Jenkins Reid

Elderly Evelyn Hugo, once a popular actress during the Golden Age of Hollywood, recounts her life to unknown reporter Monique Grant. She reminisces on her rise to stardom, her seven marriages, and the stark contrast between her public reputation and private life.


Zachary Ying and the Dragon Emperor by Xiran Jay Zhao

Chinese American Zachary Ying does everything to remain unnoticed; being the only Asian kid brings enough attention. When he is possessed by the first Chinese Emperor, Qin Shi Huang, Zach must learn to use his newfound powers to defend the world against ancient spirits from the underworld.


Heartstopper by Alice Oseman

A currently four-volume webcomic, Heartstopper follows a pair of schoolboys, Nick Nelson and Kit Connor, as they experience love, friendship, and heartbreak as high-schoolers. Volumes 1 and 2 of the series have also been adapted into a Netflix TV Series.
